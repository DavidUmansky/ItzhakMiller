About

Cantor, singer, and composer was born in Israel and educated in San Francisco California . Miller acquired his formal musical education during the years 1965-1981 in San Francisco at the music departments of the Junior High and High Schools he attended and at Antioch University where he completed a B.A. in music. Miller was trained by well known cantors in California and Israel and has served as cantor in various communities both in Diaspora and in Israel. Itzhak's special relationship with R' Shlomo and his music began in the 1960's and since then became a faithful student, both musically and spiritually.

Miller performed with R' Shlomo on many occasions during the last thirty years of R' Shlomo's life both in America and in Israel. Itzhak took part in the recording of two of R' Shlomo's record albums, "Days Are Coming" and "The Gift Of Shabbos". Perhaps most relevant to this recording is the fact that Miller had the privilege of praying alongside R' Shlomo on many Shabbats and Holidays in places such as The House of Love and Prayer in San Francisco where the first Carlebach Minyan was born, and Minyanimm that were oraganized later in Los Angeles, New York, Moshav Modiin, and Jerusalem.

In concerts, Miller performs R' Shlomo's cantorial, melodic and soulful compositions as well as known compositions of his own. The lyrical voice of Itzhak Miller can express at times pure prayer and at times great joy and is imbued with an authentic Jewish feeling and rythm. Inspired by his Rebbe, R' Shlomo, Itzhak's singing can create an atmosphere that is filled with a profound love for G-od and his Torah, for the people of Israel and the land of Israel. Yitzhak's musical concerts were broadcast live on T.V at the Israeli "Arutz Ha-Rishon" national channel during the 1990's-2000's.

The great love for R' Shlomo's music and the sprouting up of the Carlebach Minyanim all over the world has inspired the making of the first Itzhak's album "Halel" that contains sections of the known prayer that Jews prey in holidays and in the monthly Rosh-Hodesh at the start of the month. It aims to enable these many Minyanimm to conduct their services in the Carlebach style and spirit and in the way that R' Shlomo would have wanted. The disc version of the album released in 2005 and today all the songs available free at the Internet. You can listen to part of them here in the website.
